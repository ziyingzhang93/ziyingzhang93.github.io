### Pancreatic Cancer Risk Stratification Using Blood Biomarkers in Diabetic Populations

**ESMO MAP 2023 · Oral Presentation · Awarded MAP MERIT Travel Grant**

Pancreatic cancer remains one of the deadliest malignancies, partly due to its late-stage diagnosis. Emerging evidence suggests that new-onset diabetes may serve as an early signal of occult pancreatic cancer. Leveraging the large-scale AMORIS cohort from Sweden, this study investigates the predictive value of routinely collected blood biomarkers for pancreatic cancer risk in individuals with newly diagnosed diabetes and pre-diabetes. We developed a risk stratification framework that integrates clinical laboratory markers with epidemiological data, aiming to identify high-risk individuals who may benefit from targeted screening. The findings contribute toward a cost-effective, non-invasive approach for earlier detection in primary care settings.

**Methods:** Epidemiological cohort analysis, survival modelling, biomarker-based risk stratification

**Data:** AMORIS cohort (Sweden), clinical laboratory records, cancer registry linkage

### Integrative Multi-Omics Immune Profiling for Prognostic Stratification in Myelodysplastic Syndromes

**EHA 2025 · Oral Presentation · Awarded Travel Grant | ASH 2024 · Poster**

Myelodysplastic Syndromes (MDS) are a heterogeneous group of clonal haematopoietic disorders with highly variable clinical outcomes. Current prognostic scoring systems capture genomic and clinical features but largely overlook the tumour immune microenvironment. This project develops an immune-based prognostic model by integrating bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, and clinical data from international collaborators in Germany and Italy. Through multidimensional analysis of immune gene signatures, we derived an immune risk score that enhances patient stratification beyond existing models. Single-cell resolution further reveals the cellular composition underlying high- and low-risk immune phenotypes, offering potential therapeutic targets in the MDS microenvironment.

**Methods:** Bulk RNA-seq deconvolution, scRNA-seq clustering and annotation, immune signature scoring, Cox proportional hazards modelling

**Data:** Multi-centre cohorts (Germany, Italy), GEO/ArrayExpress public datasets

### Translational Bioinformatics for Drug Discovery at Janssen R&D

**Janssen Pharmaceutical (Johnson & Johnson) · 2020–2022**

During two years at Janssen's China R&D centre, this work encompassed a portfolio of translational bioinformatics projects supporting drug discovery across multiple therapeutic areas. Key contributions included the acquisition and integration of high-throughput omics datasets — spanning single-cell RNA-seq, bulk transcriptomics, and public databases — to characterise disease biology and identify potential drug targets. Internal bioinformatics applications were developed to automate repetitive analysis workflows, improving reproducibility and reducing turnaround time for project teams. Close collaboration with senior data scientists and cross-functional stakeholders ensured that computational insights were translated into actionable recommendations for pipeline decision-making.

**Methods:** scRNA-seq analysis, pathway enrichment, target identification, automated pipeline development

**Tools:** R (Seurat, Bioconductor), Python (scanpy, pandas), SQL, R Shiny
